## _**Development Charges Reserve Funds**_ 

## **Issue** 

Development charges reserve funds opening and closing balances recorded in the FIR should not be in deficit positions **. (Negative reporting on schedule 60 & 61)** 

## **Background** 

Development charges (DC) are charges imposed on land development or redevelopment projects. Fees are set out in municipal by-laws, which conform to the requirements of the Development Charges Act, 1997(DCA). Fees are normally collected by municipalities when building permits are issued. The use of these fees is restricted by the DCA which only allows the funds to be spent on specific capital works. 

For accounting purposes, as these funds by their nature are restricted in their use, municipalities do not earn the collected funds until they are spent on DC eligible capital works. Development charges collected but not earned are recorded as deferred revenue, a liability on the statement of financial position. The development charges are recognized as revenue in the year amounts are spent on qualifying capital projects. 

This treatment aligns with section PS 3100 Restricted assets and revenues of the Public Sector Accounting Handbook. 

According to PS 3100.11, “Externally restricted inflows should be recognized as revenue in a government’s financial statements in the period in which the resources are used for the purpose or purposes specified. An externally restricted inflow received before this criterion has been met should be reported as a liability until the resources are used for the purpose or purposes specified.” 

## **What is a negative DC balance and how does it happen"** 

A negative DC obligatory reserve fund balance occurs when a municipality recognizes DC revenues before it collects DC fees. For example, a municipality spends money on a new road and then records as revenue the DC fees it hopes to collect in the future to pay for the road. 

In essence, the municipality has created a receivable; an amount deemed to have been earned but not yet collected. This receivable, however, is not classified as an asset; instead the municipality has classified it as a negative liability. 

## **The problem with negative DC reserve fund balances:** 

- The objective of financial statements is to record what actually occurred during the year. It is not a planning document. Recognizing DCs as revenues before amounts have been collected is not a conservative representation of a municipality’s transactions. 

- The DCA only provides for “borrowing from the fund” not borrowing against the fund. **Borrowing from implies that moneys have already been collected.** 

- As experienced by some municipalities, “the level of development charges contemplated did not materialize”.  Premature recognition of DC revenues may falsely inflate annual results. Municipalities would record a bad debt in the year the receivable was no longer deemed collectible. 

- An additional accounting issue is that there needs to be an asset in order for it to be considered a “restricted asset” and for PS 3100 to apply. PS 3100 applies to inflows, i.e. funds that have been received by the municipality, that are restricted in nature. Receivables should be classified as assets, not as negative liabilities. 

- The Province and academics rely on the data provided in the FIR, among other sources, to analyze the fiscal health of municipalities. Incorrect reporting in the FIR can compromise analysis and decision making. 

## **Framework** 

- **Information included in the FIR should reflect the audited financial statements of the municipality.** 

- **Schedules 60 and 61 are continuity schedules of Development Charges deferred revenue reserve funds.** 

- **Accordingly, schedule 61 should include actual, not planned transactions.** 

## **FIR Reporting** 

Opening and closing development charges reserve funds balances reported on Schedule 61 should be positive. 

Balances related to the DCA reported in Column 1 of Schedule 60 under Obligatory Deferred Revenue should also include positive amounts. 

Please refer to instructions on Schedule 61 of the FIR Instructions for more information.
