## **FIR INFORMATION: BULLETIN #3 PSAB Changes** 

## **Issue** 

The Public Sector Accounting Board (PSAB) introduced a variety of standards that impact financial reporting and disclosure requirements for municipalities effective January 1, 2018. The following section briefly describes the new standards, and where to report and disclose the relevant information. 

## **BACKGROUND** 

PS 2200 Related Party Disclosures: This new Section defines a related party and establishes the requirement to disclose related party transactions. Disclosure of information about related party transactions and the relationship underlying them is required when they have occurred at a value different from that which would have been arrived if the parties were unrelated, and they have, or could have, a material financial effect on the financial statements. 

PS 3210 Assets: This new Section provides guidance for applying the definition of assets set out in Section PS 1000, Financial Statement Concepts, and establishes general disclosure standards for assets. Disclosure of information about the major categories of assets that are not recognized is required. When an asset is not recognized because a reasonable estimate of the amount involved cannot be made, the reason(s) for this should be disclosed. 

PS 3320 Contingent Assets: The new Section defines and establishes disclosures standards on contingent assets. Disclosure of information about contingent assets is required when the occurrence of the confirming future event is likely. 

PS 3380 Contractual Rights: The new Section defines and establishes disclosure standards on contractual rights. Disclosure of information about contractual rights is required including description about their nature and extent and the timing. 

PS 3420 Inter-entity Transactions: In conjunction with PS 2200 Related Party Disclosures, this new Section establishes standards on how to account for and report transactions between public sector entities that comprise a government’s reporting entity from both a provider and recipient perspective. 

## **FIR INSTRUCTIONS** 

Please utilize Schedule 83 Notes to disclose information as required by any of the new standards.
